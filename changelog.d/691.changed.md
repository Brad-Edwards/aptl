The ACES realization engine no longer treats content placement as a stand-in for evidence. Wazuh corroboration and negative boundary checks are modeled as authored ACES `evidence_requirements`, the participant terminal observation is owned by its observation boundary and the runtime observation envelope, and the participant runtime binding rides the behavior specification's `x-aptl:participant-runtime-binding` governed extension instead of being planted as content. Kali is registered as a content-capable service so a participant-visible task brief lowers to a typed realization on the existing `kali_operations` volume. The participant observation boundary is also hardened: internal database and Wazuh endpoint identities (the negative-boundary reachability refs) are kept out of the participant-visible `observable_refs`/`disclosed_refs` projection and retained only as evaluator evidence.
